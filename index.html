HYDROPONICS FARMING
One technique for growing plants without the use of soil is hydroponic gardening. Rather, it uses a nutritional solution based on water to feed the plants. Here's how it works:
  1. No Soil Required: In hydroponics, plants do not grow in soil. Instead, they receive nutrients directly from water.
  2. Nutrient Solution: The plants’ roots are submerged in a nutrient-rich water solution that contains essential elements for growth. These nutrients are dissolved in water and circulate around the roots.
  3. Precise Control: Hydroponic systems allow precise control over nutrient delivery, ensuring that plants receive the right amounts of essential nutrients.
  4. Various Methods: Hydroponics can be done using containers, rafts, or vertical systems. Some common techniques include nutrient film technique (NFT), deep water culture (DWC), and aeroponics.
  5. Benefits: Hydroponics offers several advantages, such as faster growth, higher yields, reduced water usage, and the ability to grow crops in areas with poor soil quality.
  6. Crops: A wide variety of vegetables and florist crops can be grown successfully using hydroponic systems, including lettuce, tomatoes, and strawberries.

4 MAIN TYPES OF
HYDROPONICS SYSTEM

 Deep Water Culture (DWC) Systems:

In this straightforward system, plant roots are fully submerged in a nutrient-rich solution. Oxygenation and nutrient absorption occur efficiently. DWC is commonly used for growing lettuce and other plants that can float freely in the nutrient solution.

Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) Systems:

NFT involves a thin film of nutrient solution flowing over the roots, allowing them to absorb nutrients. It’s suitable for smaller plants and herbs.

Drip Irrigation Systems:

Drip systems deliver nutrient-rich water directly to the base of each plant through tubes or pipes. This method is versatile and widely used in both commercial and home hydroponics setups.

Aeroponics Systems:

Aeroponics suspends plant roots in the air, misting them with a nutrient solution. It’s highly efficient and allows for optimal oxygenation and nutrient absorption. NASA has even used aeroponics for space research.
